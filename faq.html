What is ISO certification and why do I need it?

ISO (International Organization for Standardization) certification is a globally recognized standard that demonstrates your company's commitment to quality, safety, environmental responsibility, or information security. It helps you:

  • Build customer trust and credibility
  • Improve operational efficiency
  • Access new markets and customers
  • Reduce costs through better processes
  • Demonstrate regulatory compliance

  • ISO 45001:2018 - Occupational Health & Safety
  • ISO 50001:2018 - Energy Management Systems
  • ISO 37001:2016 - Anti-bribery Management Systems
  • ISO 22000:2018 - Food Safety Management

How long does the ISO certification process take?

The timeline varies based on several factors:

  • Small businesses (under 50 employees): 3-6 months
  • Medium businesses (50-500 employees): 4-8 months
  • Large corporations (500+ employees): 6-12 months

Factors affecting timeline:

  • Current state of your documentation
  • Complexity of your operations
  • Staff availability for implementation
  • Regulatory requirements in your industry

Our fast-track process helps most clients achieve certification within 8-12 weeks on average.

What documents do I need to provide for ISO certification?

We'll guide you through preparing the necessary documentation. Generally, you'll need:

  • Company Registration Documents: Business registration, PAN card, GST registration
  • Organizational Structure: Company hierarchy, department details, role descriptions
  • Quality Manuals/Policies: Quality policy, objectives, procedures for key processes
  • Process Documentation: Flowcharts, work instructions, forms and templates
  • Performance Records: Internal audit reports, management review meetings, corrective actions
  • Training Records: Employee training certificates and attendance records

Don't worry if you don't have everything organized - our consultants will help you prepare and organize all required documents.

What happens during the ISO audit process?

The ISO certification audit typically has two phases:

Phase 1 - Documentation Review (1 day)

The lead auditor reviews all your documentation and processes. They verify that your management system is properly designed and implemented. No major findings expected at this stage.

Phase 2 - Implementation Audit (2-3 days)

The audit team conducts on-site verification:

  • Interviews with key personnel
  • Observation of processes in action
  • Review of records and evidence
  • Checking compliance with ISO requirements

Minor observations are noted during the audit and must be corrected before certification.

After the audit:

  • We'll receive the audit report within 10-14 days
  • If minor observations exist, we'll help you implement corrective actions
  • A follow-up verification audit may be required
  • Upon successful resolution, you receive your ISO certificate (valid for 3 years)

Do I need to stop my operations during the audit?

No, you don't need to stop your operations! In fact, the audit team prefers to see your systems working normally.

What to expect:

  • Schedule the audit** around your business hours
  • Provide access to relevant areas and personnel
  • Show real processes and documentation in use
  • Be prepared to answer questions about your management system

We'll prepare you thoroughly so the audit process runs smoothly with minimal disruption to your business.
